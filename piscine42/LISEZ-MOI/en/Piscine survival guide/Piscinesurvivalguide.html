Piscine survival guide

If you are here, it is because you have successfully logged into a workstation using your login and password. Congratulations, you have already taken the first step!

Now let's get serious.

Step 1 : Log in to Slack

The most important tool in your Piscine is Slack; it will allow you to communicate with other candidates and us. To find it, click on the search tool at the top right of your screen and follow the GIF below!

Step 2 : Open Chrome

Since the Intra is optimized for Chrome, we will be using this web browser. To find it, click on the search tool at the top right of your screen.

Now, go on https://signin.intra.42.fr/users/sign_in and use your credentials to log in.

Step 3 : Discover Intra

You will arrive on a page like this. Here are some details about Intra.

  1. Home takes you back to your student profile.
  2. Projects page offers recommended projects as well as a list of available projects.
  3. Video tab offers several instructional videos. Please note that these videos are no longer entirely up to date. It's best to use YouTube links, provided with the topics. Although some elements have changed since their inception, they will provide additional clarification.
  4. StackOverflow forum is a bit of a developer's bible. Create an account to find answers or ask your questions.
  5. Compagnies page is used for internship offers, unnecessary during the Piscine.
  6. You will find all the 42 Québec rules here; please read this page carefully!
  7. Wallet shop (virtual money of 42), also useless in Piscine.
  8. Summary table
  1. Wallet : The Wallets you own
  2. Evaluation points : Number of correction points available
  3. Cursus: Curriculum currently followed
  4. Grade : Current rank
  5. ETEC : Date of the end of your course
  1. Events calendar
  2. Evaluations table
  1. Hide : reduce ratings visually
  2. Feedback logs : To see the comments of your markers
  3. Manage slots : Allows you to select time slots to be an evaluator
  1. Workstation number on which you are connected
  2. Holygraphe is a graph of the projects available on the Cursus
  3. Edit will be used to modify your personal information
  4. Broadcast forwards all important announcements
  5. Login and drop-down menu provide access to your personal information

Step 4 : Your first project !

To start your projects, click on tab number 2, the projects page, and subscribe to modules

“shell 00” and “shell 01”. Once on that page, click on “register”.

And “see my project page” to be redirected to your project page.

You will find the subjects, videos, and resources needed to complete the exercises on the menu on the left.

The Git repository will be the school server repository. It is with this link that you can send your work for evaluation.

Talking is good, coding is better

It's up to you to open the Subject for Shell 00 and start reading.

To start the first exercise, all you have to do is watch the videos and open a terminal. For this, as for opening Chrome, just type iterm.

Get a GIT !

Now that you have opened a terminal, the best thing is to create your Git folder, where you will put all your exercise files. To help you, documentation is available on the Internet, and videos are available on the Youtube channel of 42.

Step 5 : I’m done !!

Once your first project is done, you will need a SSH key to Push your work on the servers. For that, Google is your friend ! Look for ssh_keygen.

Once your SSH key created, you need to install it on the Intra.

Happy Piscine, and may the odds be with you !
